Finished PCB and Designs
I finished the routing for everything. This took the longest time, and I was stressing out about how close the decoupling capacitors had to be to the things they were decoupling. The ground fill also took a while, the fill didn't completely fill the entire thing and I put some stitching vias but still it gave me issues, so I began connecting grounds via traces and vias, but then I realized that you could just reduce the clearance settings for the fill and after tinkering around I got it working. There's no thermal errors except for the USB port, which I figured is probably going to be fine as there's not much I can do, I can't change the design of the receptacle. All the components are connected properly and I made sure to leave a bit more spacing between some parts than the tutorial did because I will be hand soldering them. I then made some cool designs and made a new name for the project, the "Potato Pi".
